Exercises

Actividad 1: Vocabulary

Exercise 1 – Match the Term
Match each AVT term to its correct definition:
1. Subtitling
2. Voice-over
3. Audio description
4. Dubbing
5. Localization
Definitions:
a) Replacing the original voices with voices in another language.
b) Translating and adapting content to a specific culture or region.
c) A narrator’s voice is added over the original audio, usually at lower volume.
d) Text appears on screen translating the spoken dialogue.
e) Spoken explanations of visual elements for blind or visually impaired audiences.
Exercise 2 – Fill in the Gaps
Complete the sentences with the correct AVT term:
1. The film was adapted for Spanish audiences using __________ so actors’ voices were
replaced.
2. Netflix often uses __________ to make foreign shows accessible without replacing voices.
3. An __________ is essential for blind audiences because it explains what happens visually.
4. Translators must pay attention to __________ when creating subtitles so they are not too
fast to read.
5. Cultural jokes sometimes need __________ so the audience can understand them.
Exercise 3 – Word Formation
Complete the sentences with the correct form of the word in brackets:
1. Good __________ (TRANSLATE) is essential to make audiovisual products accessible
worldwide.
2. The film industry needs professionals with strong __________ (LOCALIZE) skills.
3. Subtitles must be short and clear to guarantee good __________ (READ).
4. Dubbing requires perfect __________ (SYNCHRONIZE) between voice and lip movements.
5. Cultural __________ (ADAPT) is sometimes necessary when jokes or idioms don’t work in
another language.
6. Accessibility services, such as audio __________ (DESCRIBE), are important for blind
audiences.
7. The voice actors showed great __________ (CREATE) when interpreting the characters.
8. Clear __________ (COMMUNICATE) is key when translating idiomatic expressions.
